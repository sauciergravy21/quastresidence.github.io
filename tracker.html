Greenbrier Reserve Fund: The Path to Stability

Analysis based on the August 2025 Reserve Study (Ref: 240731) by Building Reserves Inc.

💡 The Core Issue: We Are Funding Below Expert Recommendations

According to the official Reserve Study, the association is currently funding reserves at only 64% of the recommended minimum. While the Board has been prudent, the math shows a dangerous gap between our current contributions and the cost of future repairs.

The Current Reality:

  • Current Balance: $148,673 (Only 5.06% of what is needed to be "fully funded").
  • Current Contribution: $55,022/year (~$57/unit/month).
  • The Gap: We are short by nearly $2.8 Million in projected reserves.

The Critical Warning: Even if we follow the study's "Recommended Plan" exactly, the fund will crash to 3.7% funded in 2037. This triggers a massive special assessment crisis or financial insolvency. We need a new strategy to avoid that cliff.

📊 The Funding Gap: Current vs. Recommended

⚠️ The Gap Widens Over Time: The study recommends starting at $86,200 in 2026, rising to $367,000 by 2035. Our current flat contribution of $55k leaves us falling further behind every year.

📉 The 2037 Cliff: Why the "Recommended" Plan Isn't Enough

🚨 The Problem with the Status Quo: Following the study's minimum recommendation (Red Line) leads to a crash in 2037.

💡 The Stability Path (Blue Line): To reach a healthy 70% funded status and avoid special assessments, we must accelerate contributions. This isn't about "perfect" funding; it's about avoiding a financial collapse.

🛣️ A Phased Approach to Stability

Instead of a sudden shock, we propose a gradual ramp-up to reach the "Stability Target" over 10 years.

1

Immediate Adjustment (2026)

Increase contributions to $86,200/year ($89.79/unit/month). This aligns with the study's starting point and stops the bleeding immediately.

2

Gradual Ramp-Up (2027–2030)

Incrementally increase contributions by ~15-20% annually. This spreads the cost over time, making it manageable for owners while building the buffer.

3

Target Stabilization (2031+)

Aim for the $500,000/year target (~$520/unit/month) by 2035. This ensures we hit the 70% funded mark and eliminate the risk of a 2037 special assessment.

*Note: The $500k figure represents the "Ideal Stability" scenario. The absolute minimum to avoid the 2037 crash is slightly lower, but requires accepting a lower funded percentage for longer.

Source: Building Reserves Inc., "August 2025 Reserve Study for Greenbrier Condominium Association Six", Ref: 240731. Data as of June 30, 2025.

Greenbrier Reserve Fund: The 2037 Survival Plan

Analysis based on the August 2025 Reserve Study (Ref: 240731) by Building Reserves Inc.

💡 The Goal: Avoid the 2037 Financial Collapse

We aren't aiming for "perfect" funding. We are aiming for survival. The Reserve Study projects that if we continue on our current path, our fund approaches depletion in 2037 when major repairs are due.

The Reality:

  • Current Status: We are funding at only 64% of the recommended minimum.
  • The Risk: The Reserve Study flags 2037 as the "Critical Year." That single year brings over $1.6 million in expenses:
EPDM Roof Replacement $1,089,950
Plumbing Riser Sections (phased) $186,062+
Carpet Replacement $126,988
Various Other Projects ~$200,000+
2037 Total $1,600,000+

With only $148,673 currently in reserves, that math doesn't work without either massive contribution hikes or special assessments.

  • The Target: We need to build a buffer that allows us to pay the 2037 bills and still have 30-40% funded remaining. This is the "Safety Floor."

Why This Matters: Hitting the Safety Floor means no special assessments for owners in 2037. It means we pay for the roof and elevators with cash we already saved, not debt or emergency fees.

📊 The Funding Gap: Current vs. Survival Target

⚠️ The Gap: The study recommends starting at $86,200 in 2026. To hit our "Survival Target" (30-40% post-2037), we need to ramp up to approximately $380,000/year by 2035.

📉 The 2037 Cliff: Current Path vs. Survival Path

✅ The Win Condition:
1. Red Line (Current Path): Fund drops to critically low levels in 2037. Disaster.
2. Green Line (Survival Path): We ramp up contributions to ensure that after the 2037 expenses, we land at 30-40% funded.

This isn't about being perfect. It's about ensuring we don't go bankrupt.

💡 Owner Action: Putting Equity First

Understanding the math is one thing; acting on it is another. One Greenbrier owner has already taken personal action to address the current $30k shortfall.

"I am voluntarily covering my prorated share of the identified shortfall—writing checks for 10 months of my portion—solely to protect the equity in my unit. I don't want my property value to suffer because we waited too long to fix the math. This isn't about HOA fees; it's about asset protection."

This proactive approach demonstrates that the numbers are real and urgent. Protecting your investment starts with acknowledging the reality of the reserve gap.

🛣️ The Survival Roadmap

A phased, realistic approach to building the buffer needed to survive 2037.

1

Immediate Adjustment (2026)

Increase contributions to $86,200/year ($89.79/unit/month). This aligns with the study's baseline and stops the immediate bleed.

2

Gradual Ramp-Up (2027–2034)

Incrementally increase contributions to reach the $380,000/year target (~$395/unit/month). This spreads the cost over 8 years, making it manageable for owners.

3

2037: The Survival Test

Over $1.6 million in bills come due — roof, plumbing, carpet, and more. Pay them using the accumulated reserve. Result: The fund retains a healthy balance. We retain a 30-40% cushion to handle the next cycle. No special assessments.

*Note: The $380k target is the "Survival Minimum." Going higher (towards $500k) would be ideal, but $380k is the threshold to avoid the 2037 crisis.
